About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist, who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He is regarded as the Father of the Nation in India and is also called Bapu (Gujarati: endearment for father, papa) in India.

Gandhi's life and teachings have inspired many others around the world, and he continues to be remembered as one of the most influential figures in human history.
